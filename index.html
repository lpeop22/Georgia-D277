Welcome To Georgia

Georgia's history is a tale of resilience and change, spanning from its indigenous roots to its pivotal role in shaping American democracy. Established in 1733 as the last of the original 13 British colonies, Georgia quickly became a powerhouse in agriculture, particularly in cotton production. The state's involvement in the Revolutionary War and the Civil War left enduring marks on its landscape and identity. In the mid-20th century, Georgia emerged as a key battleground in the Civil Rights Movement, with Atlanta serving as a hub of activism led by figures like Martin Luther King Jr. Today, Georgia thrives as a diverse and dynamic state, boasting a vibrant culture, booming economy, and stunning natural landscapes ranging from coastal plains to mountain ranges.

Fun Fact

Georgia is the birthplace of the iconic drink known as "sweet tea". This southern beverage, beloved for its refreshing sweetness, originated in Georgia during the early 19th century. It's said that tea plants were first brought to the state in the late 1700s, and by the mid-1800s, sweet tea had become a popular drink in Georgia households. Today, sweet tea is a staple of southern cuisine and a favorite beverage across the United States.
