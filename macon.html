Macon

Macon, Georgia, founded in 1823, thrived as a commercial center along the Ocmulgee River. Known as the "Heart of Georgia," its rich musical heritage produced legends like Little Richard and Otis Redding. The city's historic architecture, including the iconic Hay House, and prehistoric sites like the Ocmulgee National Monument, showcase its diverse cultural legacy, making Macon a captivating destination for history buffs and music enthusiasts alike.

  • Population: 200,000
  • Region: Southeast
  • Average Income: $60,000
  • Classification: City
  • Year Founded: 1823
