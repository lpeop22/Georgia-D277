Macon

Macon, Georgia, founded in 1823, thrived as a commercial center along the Ocmulgee River. Known as the "Heart of Georgia," its rich musical heritage produced legends like Little Richard and Otis Redding. The city's historic architecture, including the iconic Hay House, and prehistoric sites like the Ocmulgee National Monument, showcase its diverse cultural legacy, making Macon a captivating destination for history buffs and music enthusiasts alike.

Population: 155,783
Region: Piedmont region
Average Income: $68,361
Classification: Rural
Year Founded: 1823

Fun Fact

A fun fact about Macon, Georgia is that it's known as the "Cherry Blossom Capital of the World." Each spring, Macon's streets burst into bloom with thousands of cherry blossom trees, creating a stunning pink canopy throughout the city. Macon hosts the International Cherry Blossom Festival, celebrating this natural spectacle with events like concerts, parades, and food festivals.
