Columbus

Columbus, Georgia, founded in 1828, thrived as a trade center due to its strategic location along the Chattahoochee River. It became a leading textile hub in the South. With a significant role in both the Civil War and the Civil Rights Movement, Columbus witnessed battles and activism. The city's landscape, defined by the riverfront and historic architecture, blends history with modernity. Today, Columbus is a dynamic city known for its cultural attractions and outdoor offerings, embodying a rich history and a vibrant community spirit.

  • Population: 200,000
  • Region: Southeast
  • Average Income: $60,000
  • Classification: City
  • Year Founded: 1828
