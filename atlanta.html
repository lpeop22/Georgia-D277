ATLANTA

Atlanta, Georgia, founded in 1837 as a railroad junction, has evolved into a vibrant cultural and economic center. Despite the destruction it faced during the Civil War, Atlanta emerged as the capital of Georgia and a symbol of Southern resilience. The city played a pivotal role in the Civil Rights Movement, with landmarks like the Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site bearing testament to its legacy. Today, Atlanta's diverse neighborhoods, modern skyline, and lush parks make it a dynamic destination nestled in the foothills of the Appalachian Mountains, drawing visitors with its blend of history and innovation.

Population: 499,127

Fun Fact

Atlanta, Georgia is the birthplace of Coca-Cola. In 1886, pharmacist Dr. John Pemberton created the first batch of Coca-Cola syrup in his backyard. Today, the city hosts the World of Coca-Cola museum, showcasing the history of this iconic beverage.
